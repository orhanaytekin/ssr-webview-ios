Problem:

$$\text{Area}=\sqrt{300},\angle C=60^\circ,BC=x+3,CA=x$$

Solution:

Calculate the length of AB using the area formula for triangles

Using the area formula for triangles with sine
Use the formula for the area of a triangle with sine.
\text{Area}=\frac{1}{2}ab\sin C
[\text{Area}=\frac{1}{2}ab\sin C]
Substitute the given values into the formula.
\sqrt{300}=\frac{1}{2}(x+3)x\sin 60^\circ
[\text{Area}=\frac{1}{2}ab\sin C]
[\sqrt{300}=\frac{1}{2}(x+3)x\sin 60^\circ]
Simplify using \sin 60^\circ=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}.
\sqrt{300}=\frac{1}{2}(x+3)x\cdot\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
[\sqrt{300}=\frac{1}{2}(x+3)x\sin 60^\circ]
[\sqrt{300}=\frac{1}{2}(x+3)x\cdot\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}]
Multiply and simplify the equation.
\sqrt{300}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(x^2+3x)
[\sqrt{300}=\frac{1}{2}(x+3)x\cdot\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}]
[\sqrt{300}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(x^2+3x)]
Clear the square root and simplify.
300=\frac{3}{4}(x^2+3x)
[\sqrt{300}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(x^2+3x)]
[300=\frac{3}{4}(x^2+3x)]
Multiply both sides by 4/3 to solve for x.
400=x^2+3x
[300=\frac{3}{4}(x^2+3x)]
[400=x^2+3x]
Rearrange into a quadratic equation.
x^2+3x-400=0
[400=x^2+3x]
[x^2+3x-400=0]
Solve the quadratic equation using the quadratic formula.
x=\frac{-3\pm\sqrt{3^2-4\cdot1\cdot(-400)}}{2\cdot1}
[x^2+3x-400=0]
[x=\frac{-3\pm\sqrt{3^2-4\cdot1\cdot(-400)}}{2\cdot1}]
Calculate the discriminant and solve for x.
x=\frac{-3\pm\sqrt{1609}}{2}
[x=\frac{-3\pm\sqrt{3^2-4\cdot1\cdot(-400)}}{2\cdot1}]
[x=\frac{-3\pm\sqrt{1609}}{2}]
Approximate the positive solution for x.
x\approx15
[x=\frac{-3\pm\sqrt{1609}}{2}]
[x\approx15]
Calculate AB using x.
AB=x+3=15+3
[x\approx15]
[AB=18]
