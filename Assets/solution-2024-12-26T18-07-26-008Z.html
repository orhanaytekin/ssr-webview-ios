Problem:

$$\text{Area}=\sqrt{300},\angle C=60^\circ,BC=x+3,CA=x$$

Solution:

Using the formula for the area of a triangle with sine

Using the formula for the area of a triangle with sine
Use the area formula for a triangle with sine.
The area of a triangle is given by \( \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C \).
[\frac{1}{2}(x+3)x\sin 60^\circ=\sqrt{300}]
Substitute \( \sin 60^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \).
Substitute \( \sin 60^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \) into the equation.
[\frac{1}{2}(x+3)x\sin 60^\circ=\sqrt{300}]
[\frac{1}{2}(x+3)x\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}=\sqrt{300}]
Simplify the equation.
Simplify to get \( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(x^2+3x)=\sqrt{300} \).
[\frac{1}{2}(x+3)x\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}=\sqrt{300}]
[\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(x^2+3x)=\sqrt{300}]
Clear the square root by squaring both sides.
Square both sides to eliminate the square root.
[\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(x^2+3x)=\sqrt{300}]
[\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(x^2+3x)\right)^2=300]
Solve for \( x \).
Solve the equation \( \frac{3}{16}(x^2+3x)^2=300 \).
[\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(x^2+3x)\right)^2=300]
[x^2+3x=20]
Factor the quadratic equation.
Factor \( x^2+3x-20=0 \) to find \( x \).
[x^2+3x=20]
[(x-5)(x+4)=0]
Find the positive solution for \( x \).
The positive solution is \( x=5 \).
[(x-5)(x+4)=0]
[x=5]
Calculate \( AB \) using \( x \).
Substitute \( x=5 \) into \( AB=x+3 \).
[x=5]
